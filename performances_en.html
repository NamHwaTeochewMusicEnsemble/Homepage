Lady Moon

The Mid-Autumn Festival is a traditional Chinese festival. In Teochew area of China, "Moon Worship" is an indispensable part of the Mid-Autumn Festival. Teochew people also call it “Workshipping The Lady Moon”, the moon is Yin, called Taiyin Niang, folk called it “Yue Niang”, which means “Lady Moon”. And “Workshipping The Lady Moon” is actually worshipping “Chang-E”, the goddess of the moon. In this way, people pray for family reunions throughout the year, good health, and harmony.

Nam Hwa Blazes: The Sound of Teochew Music

The May Dragon Boat

The piece brings together the Teochew string ensemble ‘May Fifth’, accompanied by the vigorous gongs and drums of dragon boat racing. It also incorporates an adapted Minnan song ‘Burning Rice Dumplings’, performed in the Teochew light six-tone style with the addition of double percussion, giving the entire composition a stronger festive atmosphere of the Dragon Boat Festival.”
